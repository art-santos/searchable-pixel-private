# Why AI Engines Aren't Citing Your Content (And How to Fix It)

Many site owners are asking: "Why isn't my content getting cited by ChatGPT or other AI engines?" The answer often lies in domain trust and authority. Unlike traditional SEO where backlinks drive ranking, AI search engines evaluate your site's expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness (E-E-A-T) to determine citation-worthiness.

In this guide, you'll learn actionable strategies to boost your domain's trust signals specifically for AI engines like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity.

## Understanding E-E-A-T for AI Citations

Google's E-E-A-T guidelines have evolved beyond traditional SEO signals into crucial factors that determine whether AI engines will cite your content. Let's break down what AI systems are actually looking for:

## The Four Trust Pillars That Make AI Engines Cite You

### 1. Experience: Show, Don't Just Tell

AI engines prioritize content that demonstrates genuine, first-hand experience:

- **Case studies with measurable results**: "We increased conversion rates by 37% using this technique"
- **Practical implementation details**: "Here's the exact code we used to solve this problem"
- **Testing data**: "We tested 5 different approaches; here's what performed best"
- **Direct observations**: "During our 6-month implementation, we discovered three critical factors..."

**Pro tip**: Include specific metrics, timelines, and documented processes to signal real experience with your subject matter.

### 2. Expertise: Establish Credibility Signals

AI systems evaluate expertise through multiple signals:

- **Author credentials**: Professional qualifications, degrees, certifications
- **Work history**: Relevant industry experience (years, companies, roles)
- **Publication record**: Books, papers, articles, patents
- **Specialized knowledge**: Technical depth demonstrated through content

**Implementation example**:

### 3. Authoritativeness: Build Recognition Signals

AI engines look for external validation of your authority:

- **Citations from trusted sources**: Academic papers, industry reports
- **Professional recognition**: Awards, speaking engagements
- **Institutional affiliations**: Universities, research organizations
- **Industry leadership**: Board positions, advisory roles

**What works**: Create content worthy of citation by providing original research, comprehensive guides, or unique insights not available elsewhere.

### 4. Trustworthiness: The Foundation of AI Citations

Trustworthiness is the foundation that supports all other E-E-A-T elements:

- **Factual accuracy**: Verifiable information with proper citations
- **Transparency**: Clear methodologies, disclosure of limitations
- **Content freshness**: Regular updates with current information
- **Editorial standards**: Proper attribution, correction policies
